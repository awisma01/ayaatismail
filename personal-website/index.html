What I
Work On

Palestinian Women's Cinema

I study how Mai Masri, Annemarie Jacir, and Farah Nabulsi use formal cinematic strategies — camera angle, duration, ellipsis, offscreen sound — to refuse rather than reproduce the surveillance gaze they are forced to document.

Key Questions:
  • How do Palestinian women filmmakers subvert surveillance?
  • What cinematic techniques are used to resist occupation?
  • How does film become a tool for feminist witnessing?
Featured Films: 3000 Nights, Salt of this Sea, The Present

Counter-Cartographic Practice

Drawing on de Certeau, Lefebvre, and Weizman, I theorize Palestinian cinema as a form of spatial argumentation: a way of narrating the land from within rather than surveying it from above.

Key Concepts:
  • Counter-mapping and spatial resistance
  • Everyday navigation of borders and checkpoints
  • Film as a map of lived experience
Featured Analysis: Checkpoint scenes, landscape shots, movement and stasis

Surveillance, Race, and Gender

Using Simone Browne's framework of racialized surveillance and Shalhoub-Kevorkian's concept of infiltrated intimacies, I analyze how occupation enters domestic, bodily, and intimate space — and how filmmakers witness from inside those spaces.

Key Themes:
  • Intersectionality in surveillance studies
  • Gendered experiences at checkpoints
  • Resistance through everyday acts
Featured Work: Interviews, oral histories, and autoethnography

Digital Humanities Methodology

My digital work explores how the form of an archive can enact its own argument — building platforms where geography, film analysis, and theoretical vocabulary exist in conversation rather than sequence.

Key Projects:
  • Interactive digital archives
  • StoryMaps and spatial storytelling
  • Collaborative, open-access scholarship
Featured Platform: Cinematic Refusals

Cinematic
Counter-Cartographies

Cinematic Counter-Cartographies

A digital archive mapping how Palestinian women filmmakers transform Israeli checkpoints and surveillance infrastructures into sites of cinematic resistance across twenty-five years of film.

  • Archive Period: Palestinian Cinema, 2000–2025
  • Primary Filmmakers: Masri, Jacir, Nabulsi
  • Checkpoints Mapped: Qalandiya · Huwwara · Rachel · Container
  • Theoretical Framework: Sumud · Counter-Cartography · Feminist Witnessing
  • Languages: English · Arabic (Palestinian dialect)

Who
I Am

I am a PhD student and CHI Fellow at Michigan State University, specializing in digital humanities, Palestinian film studies, and feminist surveillance theory. My research explores the intersection of cinema, space, and resistance, with a focus on Palestinian women's filmmaking and counter-cartographic practices.

I am passionate about building digital archives and platforms that challenge dominant narratives and create space for marginalized voices. My work is grounded in feminist methodologies and a commitment to collaborative, community-centered scholarship.
