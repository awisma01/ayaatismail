Cinematic Counter-Cartographies

A digital archive mapping how Palestinian women filmmakers transform Israeli checkpoints and surveillance infrastructures into sites of cinematic resistance across twenty-five years of film.

  • Platform: GitHub Pages (static site), HTML/CSS/JS, ArcGIS mapping embeds
  • Archive Period: Palestinian Cinema, 2000–2025
  • Primary Filmmakers: Masri, Jacir, Nabulsi
  • Checkpoints Mapped: Qalandiya · Huwwara · Rachel · Container
  • Theoretical Framework: Sumud · Counter-Cartography · Feminist Witnessing
  • Languages: English · Arabic (Palestinian dialect)

A Palm Oil Project

A Palm Oil Project is a collaborative digital humanities project (with April Best and Jambi students) that explores the global, environmental, and social impacts of palm oil production. Using digital mapping and storytelling, the project traces the histories, labor, and ecologies entangled with palm oil in Indonesia and beyond.

  • Platform: ArcGIS StoryMaps
  • Focus: Environmental justice, labor, and global commodity chains
  • Collaboration: April Best, Ayaat Ismail, & graduate students from Jambi University, Indonesia
  • Jambi Students (COIL group): Lindang Mei Dwi Sariningrum, M. Aris Munandar, Puputri, Annisa Putri Hadiningsih, Annisa Putri Hadiningsi
  • Date: March 2024
