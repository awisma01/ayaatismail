Pedagogy

My teaching philosophy is rooted in feminist, anti-racist, and student-centered pedagogies. I design courses that foreground critical thinking, creativity, and collaborative inquiry, with a focus on digital humanities, film, and global studies. My classroom is a space for students to explore intersections of identity, power, and representation, and to develop skills for both academic and public scholarship.

  • Courses Taught: Introduction to Women Authors, Introduction to Women’s and Gender Studies, Race & Cinema, Monsters, African American Literature, College Writing, Editing & Publishing
  • Pedagogical Interests: Digital storytelling, counter-archives, collaborative projects, inclusive assessment, public humanities
